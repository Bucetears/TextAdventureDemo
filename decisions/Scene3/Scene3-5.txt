“May I don’t know what you get for bringing that up, but that’s not my life anymore. It might as well not have even happened.”
She stared at him silently, before leaning close to him so she could whisper in his ear. “Even if you say that, it still did happen. No matter how you deny it, that part of your life you always be a part of what shaped you.”
He backed away from her and looked her in the eyes. She was smiling, but her eyes weren’t. What’s up with her saying things that make it sound like she actually can think?
“Does it make you want to rip it to shreds?” She continued to whisper as if she was sharing some kind of secret. “Tear yourself up so that there is nothing that can remind you of what happened before.”
He wanted to just say no, but that didn’t feel right in this context.
